Our Structure
Organizational model

A three-tier structure connecting grassroots action to continental leadership.

GAP operates through Stations, Powerhouses, and Citadels — each level with distinct roles, governance responsibilities, and accountability to the communities and mission they serve.

Level 01

GAP Stations

Stations are the heartbeat of the GAP network. They are local chapters founded and led by young people in cities, universities, and communities across Africa.

What is a Station?

A GAP Station is a locally organized group of young leaders committed to implementing GAP's vision, values, and programs in their city or campus. It is the primary point of contact between GAP and the communities it serves.

Roles & Governance

Each Station is led by a founding team including a Station Leader, Deputy, Secretary, and Program Coordinators. The team is elected by Station members and accountable to the community and the Powerhouse above them.

Responsibilities

  • Implement GAP programs locally
  • Recruit and onboard members
  • Organize community events and projects
  • Report to the Powerhouse quarterly
  • Uphold GAP values and ethical standards

Membership & Size

Each GAP Station is composed of 15 to 20 active members. This size ensures cohesion, accountability, and the ability to effectively deliver programs at the local level.

Leadership Renewal

Station leadership serves a 1-year mandate, renewable but not consecutively — ensuring fresh leadership, broader participation, and accountability. Outgoing leaders transition to advisory or mentor roles.

Level 02

Powerhouses

Powerhouses are regional coordination hubs that connect, support, and amplify a cluster of GAP Stations within a defined geographic zone.

What is a Powerhouse?

A Powerhouse coordinates 5 to 8 Stations in a region — a province, a country, or a cross-border zone. It is the operational bridge between grassroots community work and the continental governance of the Citadel, managed by dedicated project managers.

Roles & Governance

A Powerhouse is led by a Regional Coordinator team selected from experienced Station leaders. They report to the nearest Citadel and are accountable for the performance, values, and support of all Stations in their zone.

Responsibilities

  • Coordinate and support 5 to 8 Stations in the zone
  • Facilitate regional events, trainings, and summits
  • Monitor Station performance and values alignment
  • Report to the Citadel bi-annually
  • Connect Stations to partnership and funding opportunities

Scope & Size

Each Powerhouse coordinates 5 to 8 GAP Stations, led by dedicated project managers who ensure implementation quality, alignment with GAP values, and inter-Station coordination within the zone.

Leadership Renewal

Powerhouse leadership serves a 1-year mandate, renewable but not consecutively. This promotes accountability and ensures emerging leaders across Stations can rise into coordination roles.

Project Levels in a Powerhouse

Powerhouses coordinate three categories of projects:

Local
Station-level projects

Community-specific initiatives run by individual Stations with local resources and leadership.

Regional
Powerhouse-level projects

Cross-Station initiatives requiring coordination across multiple Stations within the Powerhouse zone.

Continental
Citadel-level projects

Pan-African programs mandated by the Citadel and rolled out through the Powerhouse network to all Stations.

Level 03

Citadels

Citadels are the highest organizational authority within GAP — continental governance bodies responsible for strategic direction, institutional integrity, and pan-African representation.

What is a Citadel?

A Citadel is a continental governance node — the apex of the GAP organizational structure. Citadels set the strategic agenda, maintain the integrity of the GAP mission, and provide the highest level of institutional authority and accountability.

Composition & Roles

Citadels are composed of strategic delegates elected from Powerhouses. Each Citadel has five elected positions: Leader, Vice-Leader, Secretary, Accountant, and Logistics Officer. Decisions are made through structured deliberation with clear accountability mechanisms.

Key Responsibilities

  • Set GAP's continental strategic direction
  • Authorize new Powerhouses and their zones
  • Maintain mission alignment across all levels
  • Manage institutional partnerships and representation
  • Oversee the Pan-African summit and flagship programs

Leadership Renewal

Citadel delegates serve a 1-year mandate, renewable but not consecutively. This applies to all five elected roles, ensuring that leadership at the highest level remains fresh, accountable, and representative.

Why this structure matters

Accountability flows in all directions — not just top-down.

The GAP structure is designed so that Citadels are accountable to Powerhouses, Powerhouses to Stations, and Stations to the communities they serve. Power is earned through service, not position.

Structural values

Principles that hold the structure together across every level.

Accountability

Every level reports upward and downward with transparency and regular performance reviews.

Inclusivity

Leadership pathways are open to all — merit, service, and values are the criteria for advancement.

Renewal

Term limits and democratic elections ensure fresh energy and prevent entrenchment at every level.

Connectivity

Stations, Powerhouses, and Citadels are in constant dialogue — sharing resources, learning, and solutions.
