Engagement pathways

Four ways to engage with Grand African Projects.

Create a GAP Station

Found a GAP Station in your city, town, or university. As a Station Founder, you will lead a local chapter that implements GAP programs, recruits members, organizes events, and represents the GAP vision in your community.

  • Official GAP Station recognition
  • Founding resources and onboarding kit
  • Access to GAP network and Powerhouse support
  • Leadership development pathway
Register as Station Founder

Join an Existing Station

Find a GAP Station in your city or campus and join as an active member. Participate in leadership programs, community events, training sessions, and contribute to real projects alongside fellow young changemakers.

  • Access to all Station programs and events
  • Mentorship and leadership opportunities
  • GAP member network and community
  • Pathway to leadership roles within the Station
Register as Station Member

Book Club & Masterclasses

Join the Pan African Leadership & Governance Book Club. Participate in monthly reading sessions and expert-led masterclasses on African leadership, governance, history, and innovation — in your city or online.

  • Monthly curated reading lists
  • Expert-led masterclasses (online and in-person)
  • Discussion sessions and peer learning
  • Certificate of participation
Register for Book Club

University Chapter Coordinator

Coordinate a GAP chapter at your university. Connect students to GAP's programs, book clubs, leadership training, and continental network. Be the bridge between your campus and the broader GAP movement.

  • Coordinator recognition and resources
  • University chapter setup support
  • Access to student leadership network
  • Events and program delivery tools
Register as Coordinator
