Focus Area 01

Leadership & Youth Participation

The Challenge

African youth represent over 60% of the continent's population yet remain systematically excluded from decision-making spaces in governance, business, and civil society. Leadership capacity is underdeveloped, mentorship is scarce, and institutional pathways for young leaders are narrow.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP Leadership Development Track builds a continent-wide pipeline of ethical, capable young leaders through structured training, mentorship, and practical community engagement.

Objectives

  • Train 10,000 young leaders across Africa by 2030
  • Create mentorship pipelines in every GAP Station
  • Establish youth leadership councils in target communities

Actions

  • Leadership labs and intensive training programs
  • Civic education and public dialogue forums
  • Youth-led project incubators with mentorship

Expected Impact

  • More youth in governance and civic roles
  • Stronger community accountability mechanisms
  • A growing network of ethical, action-oriented leaders

Focus Area 02

Employment & Economic Inclusion

The Challenge

Youth unemployment in Sub-Saharan Africa hovers above 10% officially — with informal underemployment far higher. Structural barriers, lack of skills matching, and exclusion from formal economic systems lock millions of young Africans out of dignified work and entrepreneurial opportunity.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP SkillAid Economic Inclusion Program equips young people with employable, entrepreneurial, and digital skills, connecting them to economic opportunities in their local and continental contexts.

Objectives

  • Equip 5,000+ youth with market-relevant skills annually
  • Support 500+ micro-enterprises in target communities
  • Create employment linkages with private sector partners

Actions

  • SkillAid bootcamps and vocational training cohorts
  • Entrepreneurship incubators and business mentorship
  • Job placement partnerships with employers

Expected Impact

  • Reduced youth unemployment in target areas
  • Thriving youth-led businesses and cooperatives
  • More inclusive and resilient local economies

Focus Area 03

Education & Skills

The Challenge

Quality education remains inaccessible for millions of African youth, particularly in conflict-affected areas, rural zones, and displaced communities. Curricula are often disconnected from real-world needs, and learning outcomes remain low despite enrollment gains.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP Education for Leadership Program complements formal education with practical, values-based learning that prepares youth to solve real problems in their communities.

Objectives

  • Supplement education for 3,000+ youth in underserved areas
  • Develop and distribute practical learning curricula
  • Train 200+ community educators and facilitators

Actions

  • Community learning hubs and educational camps
  • Reskilling masterclasses for youth in transition
  • Educational partnerships with schools and NGOs

Expected Impact

  • Higher youth preparedness for the workforce
  • Stronger literacy, numeracy, and critical thinking
  • Communities that value and invest in learning

Focus Area 04

Access to Finance

The Challenge

Young African entrepreneurs and community innovators face near-total exclusion from formal financial systems. Lack of collateral, limited credit history, and structural banking barriers make it nearly impossible for youth to fund their ideas and businesses.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP Financial Inclusion Track connects youth to financial literacy training, seed funding opportunities, and partnerships with financial institutions and impact investors.

Objectives

  • Train 2,000+ youth in financial literacy annually
  • Facilitate seed funding for 100+ youth ventures per year
  • Build relationships with microfinance and impact investors

Actions

  • Financial literacy workshops and mentoring
  • Pitch competitions and investor matchmaking
  • Partnerships with banking institutions and DFIs

Expected Impact

  • More youth-owned businesses with sustainable funding
  • Greater financial inclusion across target communities
  • A generation that understands and manages capital

Focus Area 05

Peace, Security & Stability

The Challenge

Armed conflict, political instability, and community violence continue to displace millions of young Africans and derail development gains. Youth are both primary victims and, when excluded, potential vectors of instability.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP Peacebuilding & Community Cohesion Program trains conflict-sensitive leaders, supports dialogue processes, and delivers humanitarian relief in crisis-affected communities.

Objectives

  • Train 1,000 peace advocates and community mediators
  • Facilitate 50+ inter-community dialogue sessions annually
  • Support displaced communities with dignity-centered relief

Actions

  • Conflict-sensitive leadership training
  • Community peace dialogues and reconciliation processes
  • Humanitarian support in displacement camps

Expected Impact

  • More stable and cohesive communities
  • Youth as active peacebuilders, not bystanders
  • Reduced vulnerability in conflict-affected areas

Focus Area 06

Physical & Mental Health

The Challenge

Africa faces a growing youth mental health crisis, compounded by chronic underinvestment in health infrastructure. Trauma from conflict, poverty-related stress, and social stigma keep millions of young people without the support they need to thrive.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP Health & Wellbeing Track integrates mental health awareness, psychosocial support, and health education into community programs and leadership training.

Objectives

  • Reach 5,000 youth with mental health awareness programs
  • Train 300 community health advocates
  • Embed wellbeing practices in all GAP programs

Actions

  • Mental health awareness campaigns and workshops
  • Psychosocial support in humanitarian settings
  • Health education integrated into leadership training

Expected Impact

  • Reduced stigma around mental health in communities
  • Healthier, more resilient young leaders
  • Better health outcomes in underserved communities

Focus Area 07

Climate Resilience

The Challenge

African communities bear the heaviest burden of climate change despite contributing least to global emissions. Floods, droughts, and erratic seasons devastate livelihoods and food security, with youth and children disproportionately affected.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP Green Leadership Program trains young climate advocates, supports community adaptation strategies, and mobilizes action on environmental challenges in vulnerable communities.

Objectives

  • Train 2,000 youth as climate action leaders
  • Launch 30+ community adaptation projects
  • Advocate for youth inclusion in climate policy spaces

Actions

  • Climate awareness camps and green leadership training
  • Community-led environmental restoration projects
  • Policy advocacy and youth climate delegations

Expected Impact

  • Stronger community climate resilience
  • Youth-led green economy initiatives
  • Africa's voice in global climate negotiations
08
Focus Area 08

Digital Inclusion

The Challenge

The digital divide cuts deeply across Africa. Limited internet access, low digital literacy, and infrastructure gaps leave millions of youth unable to participate in the digital economy or access online education and opportunities.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP Digital Empowerment Track builds digital literacy, connects youth to technology tools, and equips the next generation to participate confidently in the digital economy.

Objectives

  • Train 4,000 youth in foundational digital skills
  • Establish 10 digital learning hubs in GAP Stations
  • Connect youth to online work and learning platforms

Actions

  • Digital literacy bootcamps and coding workshops
  • Online entrepreneurship and remote work readiness
  • Technology access partnerships and device programs

Expected Impact

  • Youth actively participating in the digital economy
  • More African voices in the global tech conversation
  • Reduced digital inequality across target communities

Focus Area 09

Gender Equality & Protection

The Challenge

Young African women and girls face compounded barriers — gender-based violence, unequal access to education and economic opportunity, early marriage, and exclusion from leadership. Structural inequalities persist despite policy gains.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP Gender Equity & Protection Program elevates women's leadership, advocates for gender equity in all programs, and provides protection-conscious support for vulnerable youth.

Objectives

  • Ensure 50% women and girl participation in all GAP programs
  • Train 500+ women leaders through dedicated pathways
  • Advocate for gender equity in policy and community spaces

Actions

  • Women's leadership cohorts and networks
  • Gender-sensitive program design in all tracks
  • Community awareness and protection campaigns

Expected Impact

  • More women in leadership roles across GAP and communities
  • Safer, more equitable community environments
  • Reduced gender-based discrimination in program areas

Focus Area 10

Accountability & Governance

The Challenge

Weak governance, corruption, and lack of accountability erode community trust, divert resources from public services, and discourage civic participation. Youth are often the first to experience these failures and the last to be included in solutions.

GAP's Initiative

The GAP Governance & Civic Accountability Track equips youth with the knowledge, tools, and platforms to demand and practice good governance at every level.

Objectives

  • Train 2,000 civic leaders in governance and accountability
  • Establish youth accountability monitoring networks
  • Produce 20+ community governance reports annually

Actions

  • Governance education and civic literacy programs
  • Youth-led public accountability initiatives
  • Policy engagement and advocacy training

Expected Impact

  • More transparent and accountable institutions
  • Youth as active monitors and advocates in governance
  • Stronger civic culture and public trust
