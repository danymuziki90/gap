About the program

A reading community built for leaders who want depth, not just inspiration.

The Pan-African Book Club on Leadership & Governance connects young leaders, university students, and civic actors around selected readings and expert masterclasses on African leadership, governance, and transformation. It is a structured learning community where ideas become the foundation for action.

  • Monthly curated book clubs

    Selected books and readings on African leadership, history, governance, and transformation — curated by a dedicated editorial team around a monthly theme.

  • Governance challenge discussions

    Structured peer dialogues where members reflect, debate, and apply governance and leadership ideas from readings to real African challenges.

  • Expert-led masterclasses

    Monthly sessions with academics, policymakers, practitioners, and thought leaders diving deep into leadership and governance themes relevant to Africa today.

  • Regional chapters

    Members participate in local chapters linked to GAP Stations across the continent, creating both an in-person and online Pan-African reading community.

  • Certificate of participation

    Members who complete a full cycle receive a GAP certificate recognizing their commitment to Pan-African leadership development.

Why this matters

Africa needs leaders who have thought deeply about governance, history, and transformation — not just those who act quickly.

The Book Club creates the intellectual foundation for the kind of leadership Africa needs at this moment in its history.

Program objectives

What the Book Club is designed to achieve.

Deepen leadership thinking

Develop a rigorous intellectual foundation for ethical, strategic, and community-centered leadership in Africa.

Build governance literacy

Equip participants with a clear understanding of how governance works, why it matters, and how youth can influence it.

Foster Pan-African identity

Create shared reference points and intellectual community among young leaders across the continent and diaspora.

Translate ideas into action

Connect reading and discussion to practical leadership initiatives, projects, and community engagement through GAP Stations.

Program activities

What happens in the Book Club every month.

01

Book Selection

The editorial team selects a title aligned with that month's leadership or governance theme. Members receive a reading guide with key questions.

02

Independent Reading

Members read individually over 2–3 weeks. Discussion prompts help them connect ideas to their own context and experience.

03

Group Discussion

Members gather in city chapters or online to share reflections, debate ideas, and connect the reading to African leadership realities.

04

Masterclass Session

An expert facilitates a deep-dive masterclass on the month's theme — live online or in-person with Q&A and interactive dialogue.

05

Action Reflection

Members identify one concrete action inspired by the month's theme to implement in their community, workplace, or Station.
